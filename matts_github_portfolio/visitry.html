Overview

This is a real-world problem from a live company with the opportunity to engage skills and abilities with real clients. I was randomly matched with a company and a team of students. We worked as a team contracted out through springboard as workers giving expert, hands-on consultation to a growing company that knew very little about UX/UI design. Visitry Is a small growing company that tethers the gap between licensed clinicians and their patients by utilizing different Clinician based companies and their Patients and supplying companies with clinicians who give their Patients a pleasant In-home experience.

Team Process

Competitive Research summary

The team, myself included, gathered research on the company and its competitors. In the competitive research analysis, we agreed on using UI patterns from companies like Uber to concern their friendly onboarding experience to concern its style in easy document sorting, applying a sort of gamification to give a sense of reward and achievement. These companies may have had nothing to do with Visitry as far as its market in direct competition. Still, it had everything to do with its goals and our mission to create a seamless and intuitive onboarding experience for the new clinicians of Visitry. We decided to evaluate the competitors and UI patterns of existing companies with similar goals and utilize Jakob Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics.
